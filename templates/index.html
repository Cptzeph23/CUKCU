The Cooperative University of Kenya Christian Union

The Cooperative University Christian Union is an interdenominational and non-denominational organization of christian students.

    Bible Study

    CUKCU has organized Bible study groups for students to come together and share the word of God guided by Bible study guides provided by FOCUS kenya. The groups are formed according to various places of residences within Gataka and school.

    Prayers

    For spiritual growth and personal encounter with God, CUKCU has partnerd with Redeemed Gospel church-Gataka where every evening from 7:00PM to 8:30Pm students gather at this church to pray.

    Praise and Worship.

    "God is a spirit and those who worship him must worship Him in Spirit and in truth. [John 4:24]"
    Therefore, CUKCU has designated Thursday from 4pma to 6pm and Saturday from 4pm to 6pm as days to practice songs for praise and worship during the Sunday service

    Leadership

    For better running of the church, CUKCU provides leadership roles to eligible and elected members to carry out leadership practices in various ministries and dockets

    Campus Night of Worship

    On the 9th of February, 2024, CUKCU Hostel a night of worship experirence at the Cooperative University's Room A, hosting campuses within Karen and some from within Nairobi

    • Pst. Israel Ezekia & Praise Celebration led the occation
    • CUKCU, led by Emmanuel Mwanik, the then CU chairperson, hosted the guests

    It was a night of Praises and worship where students from various universities gathered to celebrate Christ.
    Many souls were saved and students got to have a personal encounter with Christ

    Afternoon of Worship

    On Sunday 5th November, 2022, CUKCU had an afternoon of worship from 2:00pm till 5:00pm

    • An afternoon dedicated for praise, worship and hymns
    • Experienced life changing ministerial through praises
    • Repentance, Testimonies and winning souls to Christ were experienced

    The event culminated at 5:00pm with invited guests leaving at their own pleasure as students emabrked on their normal routine.
