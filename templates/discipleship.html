Discipleship Ministry

The Discipleship Ministry exists to nurture, mentor, and equip members of the Christian Union to grow in their faith and walk with Christ. Through intentional mentorship, Bible-based teaching, and life application, this ministry fosters spiritual maturity and prepares members to disciple others.

Rooted in the Great Commission, the ministry emphasizes the importance of making disciples who, in turn, disciple others, ensuring the continuous growth of the Christian Union spiritually and numerically.

Vision and Mission

Vision: To raise a generation of Christ-like disciples who impact their spheres of influence.
Mission: To guide members of the Christian Union into a deeper relationship with Christ, equipping them to live out their faith and disciple others.

Activities and Programs

  • One-on-One Mentorship: Pairing members with experienced leaders for personal discipleship.

  • Discipleship Classes: Structured teachings on foundational Christian doctrines and practical Christian living.

  • Bible Study Groups: Encouraging in-depth exploration of God’s Word in small, interactive groups.

  • Prayer Sessions: Strengthening spiritual lives through regular intercessory and personal prayer.

  • Evangelism Training: Equipping members to share the Gospel effectively in their communities.

  • Retreats and Camps: Hosting discipleship-focused gatherings for spiritual growth and fellowship.

Key Focus Areas

The Discipleship Ministry focuses on the following areas:

  • Spiritual Growth: Encouraging members to develop personal devotional lives through Bible reading and prayer.

  • Character Development: Teaching members to live with integrity and reflect Christ in their daily lives.

  • Leadership Training: Preparing members for servant leadership within the CU and beyond.

  • Multiplication: Equipping disciples to disciple others, fostering a continuous cycle of growth.

How to Join

  1. Sign up during CU ministry registration days.

  2. Attend a discipleship orientation to learn about the ministry’s mission.

  3. Participate in discipleship classes and mentorship programs.

  4. Commit to growing in your faith and discipling others.
