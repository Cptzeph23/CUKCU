Drama Ministry

The Drama Ministry is dedicated to sharing the Gospel through creative storytelling, theatrical performances, and skits.
Using the power of drama, we aim to bring biblical truths to life, inspire transformation, and deepen faith among audiences.

This ministry is a platform where members can express their God-given talents in acting, scriptwriting, and stage production while glorifying God and impacting lives.

Vision and Mission

Vision: To use drama as a powerful tool to evangelize, disciple, and transform lives for Christ.

Mission: To communicate the message of Godâ€™s love, salvation, and righteousness through theatrical arts.

Activities and Programs

  • Gospel Skits: Short performances illustrating biblical lessons and moral values.
  • Stage Plays: Full-length plays based on Bible stories or Christian themes.
  • Creative Mime: Non-verbal dramatization of biblical truths through expressive movements.
  • Outreach Performances: Presenting drama in evangelism missions and church events.
  • Scriptwriting Workshops: Training members to write Christ-centered, impactful scripts.
  • Drama Retreats: Organizing retreats for rehearsals, spiritual growth, and team bonding.

Key Focus Areas

  • Faith-Based Storytelling: Crafting performances that are scripturally sound and engaging.
  • Character Development: Training actors to reflect Christian values both on and off the stage.
  • Evangelism Through Drama: Using plays and skits as tools for spreading the Gospel.
  • Talent Nurturing: Helping members grow in acting, directing, and stage management.

Major Highlights

  • Annual Drama Festival: A showcase of powerful, Gospel-centered stage plays.
  • Drama Sunday: A service fully dedicated to ministering through drama.
  • Evangelistic Skits: Performances at outreach programs and missions.
  • Drama Trainings: Sessions to equip members with acting, directing, and stage management skills.

How to Join

  1. Register with the Drama Ministry during CU ministry sign-ups.
  2. Attend drama workshops and rehearsals.
  3. Participate in script readings and role allocations.
  4. Engage in outreach performances and drama missions.
